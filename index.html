about

I am a computer scientist-turned-biologist using computational approaches to solve challenges in human genetics. I am almost done working towards my PhD at Stanford University, and have spent my graduate career focused on developing new methods to characterize regulatory variation. I have particularly focused on rare regulatory variants that are difficult to study with traditional approaches. I have extensive experience working with large genomic data sets in order to understand the functional consequences of common and rare genetic variation, and am passionate about using next-generation sequencing data to improve the accessibility and quality of medical treatment around the globe.

portfolio

Sardinia eQTL and isoQTL browser

This web-based browser allows users to look at the association between millions of variants found in the Sardinia population with expression traits including splicing and allele-specific expression. The paper describing how this data was generated and some of the most important findings is available as a prepint on bioRxiv.

Cross-population fine-mapping browser

This browser makes it easy to see the association between gene expression and variants found in several populations that were sequenced as part of the 1000 Genomes project. Linkage disequilbirium (LD) between causal variants makes it difficult to infer the causal variant in any given population, but combining data across populations with different LD patterns can improve our ability to fine-map causal variants. This paper is currently in preparation.
Visit browser

Sex-specific eQTL browser

This browser makes it easy to explore findings from Kukurba et al 2016 regarding sex-specific regulatory elements. Such variants are more or less active depending on the sex of the carrier.
